Alzheimer's Disease Survival Analysis

In this project, we analyzed a large synthetic health dataset to identify which factors are most closely associated with Alzheimer’s diagnosis over time. Using R, we applied survival analysis methods (including Kaplan-Meier, Weibull, and Cox proportional hazards models) to explore and predict diagnosis risk based on cognitive, functional, and behavioral assessments. Along the way, we compared models, checked assumptions, and visualized survival trends by gender. This work highlights skills in statistical modeling, feature selection, and translating health data into actionable insights.

ASA DataFest 2025: Gentrification Risk Forecasting

As part of a 48-hour nationwide data challenge, my team and I analyzed a massive real estate dataset to predict which U.S. submarkets are most at risk for commercial gentrification. We developed and compared machine learning models (Logistic Regression and XGBoost), achieving strong accuracy, and built an interactive GIS dashboard using Shiny to visualize at-risk regions in real time. Our approach combined predictive modeling with thoughtful data visualization, ultimately earning us the Best Data Visualization award, as voted by faculty and alumni. This project was a real growth moment for me, reflecting not just technical skills in modeling and non-parametric analysis, but also the ability to communicate complex results clearly to a wider audience.

Willamette University Football Analysis

As project manager, I led a research initiative in partnership with Willamette University Athletics to analyze defensive football strategy using HUDL data. Working closely with coaches, I helped identify and visualize key performance metrics like pass pressures and coverage schemes on a user-friendly web platform, adaptable for every position group. Along the way, I managed team collaboration, streamlined workflows, and ensured our analysis directly supported the team’s goals. I aided in a smooth handoff to younger students for ongoing growth and worked closely with coaches to align the tool with real on-field needs.
